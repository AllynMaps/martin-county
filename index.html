Mountaintop Removal Mining in Martin County Kentucky

Explore the Changing Landscape

Lidar Point Cloud for Mining Site

Mountaintop Removal (MTR) Mining became a popular form of mining because it was cheaper and allowed for a higher percentage of coal extraction from coal seams. Martin County, KY has a long history of coal mining, and has has about 43.5 square miles of mined land.

This data was part of a research project conducted for GEO 499 to demonstrate the change in the landscape that has occured in Martin County due to MTR mining.

The contours were digitized by tracing the contours from the 1957 topographic map. The hillshade basemap was created from a DEM derived from the traced contours. Digitizing the contour lines from the 1957 topographic map was a lengthy and tedious process that took upwards of 40+ hours to complete.
